About This Site

This website was created with the purpose of promoting Siquijor Island to a broader audience. As one of the most beautiful and culturally rich islands in the Philippines, Siquijor often remains a hidden gem to many. Our goal is to provide a platform where locals and visitors alike can discover and explore the island's history, culture, attractions, and unique features.

Through this site, we aim to give everyone a chance to learn about Siquijor's mystical past, its diverse natural beauty, and its vibrant local traditions. We hope that by providing detailed and up-to-date information, we can encourage more people to visit, experience, and appreciate the wonders of Siquijor.

Whether you're a traveler looking for your next adventure or someone curious about the island's rich heritage, this website serves as a resource that brings Siquijor closer to the world. It was created to connect people with Siquijor’s unique stories, guide them to amazing destinations, and ensure that the island's beauty is recognized and cherished.

Our hope is that this platform will foster a deeper connection with Siquijor, its people, and its vibrant culture, and contribute to the island's sustainable growth through responsible tourism and local support.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

To get to Siquijor, you can take a flight to Dumaguete City (Negros Oriental), which is the closest major airport. From there, you can take a ferry to Siquijor Island. There are regular ferry services from Dumaguete to Siquijor, which take around 1 to 1.5 hours. Alternatively, you can take a ferry from other nearby islands like Cebu or Bohol.

Yes, Siquijor is generally considered a safe destination for travelers. The island is known for its peaceful atmosphere, and the locals are friendly and welcoming. Like any travel destination, it's always a good idea to take standard safety precautions, such as securing your belongings and being aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Siquijor offers a range of accommodations to suit different budgets. There are affordable guesthouses and hostels as well as mid-range hotels and resorts. On average, you can find budget options starting from PHP 500 to 1,000 per night, while mid-range accommodations can range from PHP 2,000 to 4,000. Luxury resorts will have higher rates. Food and other expenses are also quite affordable compared to major tourist destinations in the Philippines.

Siquijor has a rich folklore that includes tales of witches, healers, and mystical practices. These stories are a big part of the island's history and culture. While many people still practice traditional healing methods involving herbs and rituals, the idea of witches in the traditional sense is more of a cultural belief than a reality. Visitors are often intrigued by the island's mystical reputation, but most of these practices are rooted in the island’s spiritual heritage and should be viewed respectfully.
